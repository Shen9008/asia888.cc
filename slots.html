RTP, volatility, and what they really mean

Return to player (RTP) is a long-run average, not a guarantee for a single session. A game with a higher theoretical RTP can still feel cold in the short term, while a lower-RTP title can still pay big in a lucky hour. Volatility describes how often and how hard a game tends to swing: high volatility means fewer but bigger hits in many models, while lower volatility often means smoother, smaller wins. Read both together with the minimum bet and your own budget before you chase a feature.
